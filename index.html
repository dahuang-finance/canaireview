Can AI Replace the Peer Reviewer?

Avner Kalay  ·  James Schallheim  ·  Da Huang

We send nine program years of submissions to the Utah Winter Finance Conference (2016–2026) — 2,443 papers, double-blinded, each scored 1–5 by two human program-committee members — to six successive frontier model versions from two vendors (Anthropic Opus 4.5/4.6/4.7 and OpenAI GPT-5.1/5.4/5.5), under a fixed prompt and the same scoring scale.

Three findings. First, AI reviewers cannot identify exceptional papers and they cannot identify hopeless ones — they compress to the middle. Second, AI agreement with humans is poor even within the range it uses. Third, the gap is closing fast: distributional distance to the human reference falls by roughly 45% over three model generations.
